Definitions for finding-Focus-extension.

Extension
Definition

The specific observed aspect of the Subject under consideration, potentially a specific condition, intervention, activity, body structure, or environmental factor. For use when the observation is not the Subject as a whole. An example would be an observation related to the Subject's bipolar disorder.

Extension.extension (http://hl7.org/fhir/us/bci/StructureDefinition/shr-base-Topic-extension)
Definition

Compositional and reusable grouping of attributes that make up the clinical focus of an entry. A topic does not include contextual attributes such as the nature of the action (ordered, proposed, planned, etc...), the nature of the patient state being described (e.g., present, suspected present, absent), and the attribution of this information (the who, when, where, how, why of the information recorded).
